# Manufacturing Process System

## Context

My first professional engineering role. Lear's internal team maintained a constellation of small web apps used by operators, leads, and engineers on the plant floor. Each shift had its own quirks, and the apps had to work on the same restricted internal browsers across every plant.

## Problem

- Operator workflows varied by product line, but the apps had grown duplicative — the same form patterns and validations reimplemented in three different places.
- Browser compatibility was locked to an old baseline, which meant no relying on modern SPAs or bleeding-edge CSS.
- Data integrity mattered: mistakes on the floor have a cost.

## Approach

- Wrote new features in ASP.NET MVC with server-rendered views and progressively enhanced JS — simple, robust, and compatible with the locked-down browser baseline.
- Pulled common form and validation patterns into a shared library so new apps could be bootstrapped without reimplementing the basics.
- Added audit trails on every critical write path, so every change could be traced to a user and a shift.

## Outcome

The new apps were faster to ship and easier to support. I went from intern to the full-time developer track based on the work that landed. More importantly, it taught me how much "unsexy" things like validation reuse and audit trails matter when real people rely on what you ship.

## Stack notes

- **ASP.NET MVC 5**
- **C#**
- **jQuery + vanilla JS** for progressive enhancement
- **SQL Server** with stored procedures for audit-heavy paths
